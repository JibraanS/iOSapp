Blueberry Pancakes
20 Minutes
Super thick and fluffy blueberry pancakes! Melt in your mouth, golden brown, and bursting with blueberries.
Breakfast
Vegetarian, Kosher
2 tbsp white vinegar, 1 cup flour, 2 tbsp sugar, 1 tsp baking powder, 1/2 tsp baking soda, 1/2 tsp salt, 1 egg, 2 tbsp butter, 1 cup blueberries
Mix milk and vinegar and let sit for 2 minutes. Whisk in dry ingredients. Whisk in the egg, milk, and melted butter. Heat a nonstick pan over medium heat and add some butter. Pour 1/3 cup of batter into the pan and add a few blueberries on top. Flip when bubbling and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes. Serve with butter and maple syrup.

Maple Candied Bacon
30 Minutes
Delicious, sweet and savory bacon.
Breakfast
Gluten-Free
1 pound thick sliced bacon, 1/2 cup maple syrup, 1 tsp dijon mustard, black pepper
Preheat the oven to 400F. Line a rimmed baking sheet with foil and place a baking rack over the sheet. Arrange bacon on the sheet. In a small bowl mix the syrup and mustard. Pour over the bacon and bake for 12-15 minutes. Flip and add more of the mixture. Bake for an additional 5-10 minutes. Remove the sheet from the oven and sprinkle it with black pepper.

Sweet and Sour Meatballs
50 Minutes
An easy, delicious recipe made with ingredients you always have around the house! A definite winner! Wonderful served with rice!
Lunch
Kosher
1 pound ground beef, 1 egg, 1/4 cup bread crumbs, 1 onion (diced), 1 cup brown sugar, 3 tbsp flour, 1 1/2 cups water, 1/4 cup distilled vinegar, 3 tbsp soy sauce
In a bowl, combine the beef, egg, bread crumbs and onion. Mix thoroughly and shape into golf ball sized balls. Over medium heat, brown the meatballs and set aside. In a saucepan, combine the brown sugar, flour, water, white vinegar, and soy sauce. Add meatballs and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and let simmer, mixing for 30 minutes.

Pesto Chickpea Salad
10 Minutes
This creamy and tangy Pesto Chickpea Salad is a quick and easy lunch. Enjoy with crackers, on a sandwich, or on top of a bed of greens.
Lunch
Gluten-Free, Vegetarian, Kosher
1/4 cup mayonnaise, 1/4 cup basil pesto, 1 tbsp lemon juice, 1/8 tsp freshly cracked pepper, 2 15oz cans chickpeas, 1/4 cup red onion, 1/4 cup fresh parsley
Stir together the mayonnaise, pesto, lemon juice, and pepper in a bowl. Rinse and drain the chickpeas. Roughly chop the parsley. Finely dice the red onions. Add the chickpeas, parsley, and onion to a large bowl. Pour the dressing over the chickpeas, parsley, and onion. Stir to combine.

Tomato Herb Soup
25 Minutes
Use basic pantry staples to create this quick and easy Tomato Herb Soup.
Lunch
Gluten-Free, Vegetarian, Kosher
2 cloves garlic, 1/4 cup olive oil, 1/2 tsp dried thyme, 1/4 tsp dried rosemary, 1 pinch crushed red pepper, 1/4 tsp freshly cracked black pepper, 1 6oz can tomato paste, 1 tbsp brown sugar, 1 28oz can crushed tomatoes, 3 cups vegetable broth
Mince the garlic and add it to a sauce pot along with the olive oil and spices and turn the heat on to medium-low and saute for about 2 minutes. Add the tomato paste and brown sugar. Stir until everything is mixed and cook the mixture while stirring continuously for 3-5 minutes. Add the crushed tomatoes and vegetable broth and whisk the mixture together until smooth. Turn the heat up to medium and heat through, stirring occasionally.

Mac and Cheese
25 Minutes
This incredibly creamy and cheesy homemade mac and cheese is great on its own or as a jumping off point for your own creation.
Dinner
Vegetarian, Kosher
8 oz macaroni, 3 tbsp butter, 3 tbsp all-purpose flour, 1/2 tsp onion powder, 2 cups whole milk, 1/2 tsp salt, 8 oz block of sharp cheddar (shredded)
Cook the macaroni and drain the macaroni in a colander. Whisk and heat butter, flour, and onion powder over medium until the butter is melted and the mixture begins to bubble. After cooking the butter and flour roux, whisk in the milk. Continue to cook and whisk over medium heat until the milk begins to gently simmer. Turn the burner off and season the white sauce. Add the shredded cheddar and the pasta to the pot and stir to combine.

Pasta Puttanesca
25 Minutes
Pasta puttanesca is an original budget-friendly meal.
Dinner
Kosher
2 tbsp olive oil, 1 yellow onion, 4 cloves garlic, 3 anchovy filets, 1 28oz can of crushed tomatoes, 1/2 tsp dried basil, 1/4 tsp crushed red pepper, 1/2 cup sliced Kalamata olives, 2 tbsp capers, 1 lb spaghetti
Add the olive oil, onion, garlic, and anchovy filets to a large skillet. Sauté the ingredients over medium heat until the onions are soft and the anchovies have dissolved. Add the crushed tomatoes to the skillet, along with the basil, crushed red pepper, sliced olives, and capers. Turn the heat to low and let the sauce simmer, stirring occasionally. Boil a large pot of water and add the spaghetti for about 7 minutes. Add the cooked and drained pasta to the sauce in the skillet and stir to combine the pasta with the sauce.

Baked Ginger Salmon
30 Minutes
Topped with a quick sweet and savory glaze, this Baked Ginger Salmon is a fast, easy, and delicious way to prepare salmon.
Dinner
Gluten-Free, Kosher
1 tsp grated fresh ginger, 1 clove minced garlic, 2 tbsp brown sugar, 1 tsp soy sauce, 1/4 tsp toasted sesame oil, 1 lb fresh salmon
Preheat the oven to 425ºF. Grate about 1 tsp fresh ginger. Combine the ginger, garlic, brown sugar, soy sauce, and toasted sesame oil in a bowl. Cut the salmon into four equal portions. Place the portioned salmon on a baking sheet lined with parchment or foil, skin side down. Spread the prepared ginger glaze over the top of each piece of salmon. Bake the salmon for about 20 minutes (15 minutes for thin fillets), or until the internal temperature reaches 145ºF.
